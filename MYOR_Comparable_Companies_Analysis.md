# SECTION 6: KEY OBSERVATIONS

## 6.1 Valuation Positioning

**MYOR trades at a meaningful premium on EV-based multiples but only a slight premium on equity multiples.** This disconnect is primarily driven by:

1. **Leverage Effect**: MYOR's higher debt levels inflate Enterprise Value relative to equity value
2. **EBITDA Quality**: Lower EBITDA margins mean investors require fewer turns of EBITDA for the same equity value
3. **Earnings Support**: P/E multiple is supported by consistent profitability despite margin pressures

## 6.2 Margin Analysis

**MYOR's margin profile is the primary value detractor:**

| Margin | MYOR | Peer Median | Gap | Implication |
|--------|------|-------------|-----|-------------|
| Gross Margin | 23.0% | 37.2% | (14.2pp) | Pricing power / cost structure issues |
| EBITDA Margin | 11.1% | 18.5% | (7.4pp) | Operating leverage deficit |
| Net Margin | 8.3% | 9.6% | (1.3pp) | Lower interest burden partially offsets |

**Catalyst:** Every 100bps improvement in EBITDA margin = ~IDR 360B incremental EBITDA = +0.5x multiple potential

## 6.3 Growth vs. Value Trade-off

MYOR's growth profile is **in-line with peers** (6.2% revenue growth vs 9.9% median), but the **growth-to-margin trade-off is unfavorable**:

- **PEG Ratio** (P/E / EPS Growth): MYOR trades at 0.85x PEG vs peers suggesting reasonable value
- **EV/EBITDA-to-Growth**: MYOR's premium is harder to justify given similar growth rates

## 6.4 Balance Sheet Considerations

MYOR's **elevated leverage** (Net Debt/EBITDA 4.5x vs 1.4x median) presents:

- **Risk**: Higher financial risk in rising rate environment
- **Opportunity**: Deleveraging to 2.5x would unlock significant value
- **Cash Generation**: Current FCF yield supports debt paydown

## 6.5 Analyst Sentiment

- **Coverage**: 30 analysts (highest coverage in peer group)
- **Consensus**: BUY rating
- **Target**: IDR 2,747 (+30% upside from current)
- **Implied Forward P/E**: 14.6x (implies 20% EPS growth)

**Interpretation:** Street is pricing in margin recovery and earnings acceleration not reflected in LTM multiples.

# APPENDIX B: GLOSSARY

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **EV (Enterprise Value)** | Market Cap + Debt - Cash; represents total firm value |
| **EBITDA** | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation & Amortization |
| **P/E** | Price-to-Earnings ratio; stock price divided by EPS |
| **P/B** | Price-to-Book ratio; market cap divided by book value of equity |
| **ROE** | Return on Equity; net income divided by shareholders' equity |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital; NOPAT divided by invested capital |
| **TTM** | Trailing Twelve Months |
| **FWD** | Forward (next twelve months estimate) |
| **NM** | Not Meaningful (multiple is negative or too large) |
| **LTM** | Last Twelve Months (same as TTM) |
| **CAGR** | Compound Annual Growth Rate |
| **PEG** | P/E divided by EPS growth rate |

# APPENDIX C: METHODOLOGY NOTES

## Weighting Rationale

Peer weights were assigned based on:

1. **Business Model Similarity** (40% of weight decision)
   - Product category overlap with MYOR
   - Revenue mix comparability

2. **Scale Comparability** (30% of weight decision)
   - Market cap proximity
   - Revenue scale alignment

3. **Trading Liquidity** (20% of weight decision)
   - Average daily volume
   - Free float percentage

4. **Data Quality** (10% of weight decision)
   - Analyst coverage
   - Reporting transparency

## Multiple Selection

- **EV/EBITDA**: Primary multiple due to capital structure neutrality
- **P/E**: Secondary multiple; commonly used by local investors
- **EV/Revenue**: Tertiary multiple; useful when margins are volatile

## Adjustments Made

- Normalized for one-time items where disclosed
- Used median over mean for outlier resistance
- Applied TTM financials for consistency
